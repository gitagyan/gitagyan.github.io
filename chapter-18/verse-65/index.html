Chapter 18, Verse 65 (Bhagavad Gita 18.65)

Chapter 18: Mokṣha Sanyās Yog – Yoga through the Perfection of Renunciation and Surrender

Sanskrit Shloka

मन्मना भव मद्भक्तो मद्याजी मां नमस्कुरु।मामेवैष्यसि सत्यं ते प्रतिजाने प्रियोऽसि मे

Transliteration

man-manā bhava mad-bhakto mad-yājī māṁ namaskuru mām evaiṣhyasi satyaṁ te pratijāne priyo ‘si me

Word Meanings

mat-manāḥ—thinking of me; bhava—be; mat-bhaktaḥ—my devotee; mat-yājī—worship me; mām—to me; namaskuru—offer obeisance; mām—to me; eva—certainly; eṣhyasi—you will come; satyam—truly; te—to you; pratijāne—I promise; priyaḥ—dear; asi—you are; me—to me

Translation

Fix your mind on Me, be devoted to Me, worship Me, and bow down to Me. Thus, you will surely come to Me. I promise you this truly, for you are dear to Me.

Meaning & Commentary

In this profound verse, Shri Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, unveils the essence of the path of devotion, Bhakti Yoga. By instructing Arjuna to fix his mind and heart upon Him, Shri Krishna invites the soul to transcend worldly attachments and establish an eternal, loving relationship with the Divine. He assures every seeker that such absolute surrender is not a burden but the highest liberation, as it leads one directly into His divine presence. This sacred promise reveals the intimate affection Shri Krishna holds for His devotees, affirming that the ultimate goal of human existence is to return to His lotus feet. It is a timeless invitation for the soul to find its eternal home in the love of the Supreme Lord.
