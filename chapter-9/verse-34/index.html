Chapter 9, Verse 34 (Bhagavad Gita 9.34)

Chapter 9: Rāja Vidyā Yog – Yoga through the King of Sciences

Sanskrit Shloka

मन्मना भव मद्भक्तो मद्याजी मां नमस्कुरु। मामेवैष्यसि युक्त्वैवमात्मानं मत्परायणः

Transliteration

man-manā bhava mad-bhakto mad-yājī māṁ namaskuru mām evaiṣhyasi yuktvaivam ātmānaṁ mat-parāyaṇaḥ

Word Meanings

mat-manāḥ—always think of me; bhava—be; mat—my; bhaktaḥ—devotee; mat—my; yājī—worshipper; mām—to me; namaskuru—offer obeisances; mām—to me; eva—certainly; eṣhyasi—you will come; yuktvā—united with me; evam—thus; ātmānam—your mind and body; mat-parāyaṇaḥ—having dedicated to me

Translation

Engage your mind always in thinking of Me, become My devotee, worship Me, and offer your homage unto Me. Being completely absorbed in Me, surely you will come to Me.

Meaning & Commentary

In this profound verse, Lord Krishna offers the ultimate essence of the path of devotion, bhakti-yoga. By instructing Arjuna to fix his mind and heart exclusively upon His divine form, Lord Krishna reveals that the soul's true nature is found in eternal communion with the Supreme. This is not merely an external ritual but an internal transformation where every thought and action becomes an offering to the Divine. When a devotee makes Shri Krishna the sole focus and the ultimate destination of their existence, the shackles of karma are broken. Thus, the soul attains liberation and enters into the eternal, loving service of the Supreme Lord.
