Chapter 10, Verse 10 (Bhagavad Gita 10.10)

Chapter 10: Vibhūti Yog – Yoga through Appreciating the Infinite Opulences of God

Sanskrit Shloka

तेषां सततयुक्तानां भजतां प्रीतिपूर्वकम्। ददामि बुद्धियोगं तं येन मामुपयान्ति ते

Transliteration

teṣhāṁ satata-yuktānāṁ bhajatāṁ prīti-pūrvakam dadāmi buddhi-yogaṁ taṁ yena mām upayānti te

Word Meanings

teṣhām—to them; satata-yuktānām—ever steadfast; bhajatām—who engage in devotion; prīti-pūrvakam—with love; dadāmi—I give; buddhi-yogam—divine knowledge; tam—that; yena—by which; mām—to me; upayānti—come; te—they

Translation

To those who are ever steadfast, worshipping me with love, I give the yoga of discrimination, by which they come to me.

Meaning & Commentary

In this profound verse, Shri Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, reveals the reciprocal nature of divine grace. He promises that when a seeker anchors their consciousness in Him through constant devotion, He personally bestows the 'Buddhi-yoga'—the divine intelligence required to transcend illusion. This is not merely intellectual knowledge, but a direct illumination of the soul that clears the path to realization. By aligning one's heart with Shri Krishna, the devotee no longer relies on limited worldly perception but is guided by the light of the Lord Himself. Ultimately, this grace ensures that the seeker reaches the eternal abode of Shri Krishna, fulfilling the purpose of human existence.
