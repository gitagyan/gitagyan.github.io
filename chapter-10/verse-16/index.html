Chapter 10, Verse 16 (Bhagavad Gita 10.16)

Chapter 10: Vibhūti Yog – Yoga through Appreciating the Infinite Opulences of God

Sanskrit Shloka

वक्तुमर्हस्यशेषेण दिव्या ह्यात्मविभूतयः। याभिर्विभूतिभिर्लोकानिमांस्त्वं व्याप्य तिष्ठसि

Transliteration

vaktum arhasyaśheṣheṇa divyā hyātma-vibhūtayaḥ yābhir vibhūtibhir lokān imāṁs tvaṁ vyāpya tiṣhṭhasi

Word Meanings

vaktum—to describe; arhasi—please do; aśheṣheṇa—completely; divyāḥ—divine; hi—indeed; ātma—your own; vibhūtayaḥ—opulences; yābhiḥ—by which; vibhūtibhiḥ—opulences; lokān—all worlds; imān—these; tvam—you; vyāpya—pervade; tiṣhṭhasi—reside;

Translation

Please describe to me without reserve Your divine glories, by which You pervade all these worlds and exist within them.

Meaning & Commentary

In this verse, Arjuna recognizes Shri Krishna not merely as his friend, but as the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the source of all existence. By asking Shri Krishna to recount His glories, Arjuna seeks to internalize the vastness of the Divine, shifting his focus from the chaos of the battlefield to the infinite majesty of the Lord. For every seeker, this request serves as a gateway to meditation, as reflecting upon the Lord's omnipresence allows one to transcend the material illusion of separation. When the devotee acknowledges that Shri Krishna sustains every atom of creation, the heart begins to surrender, paving the way for true liberation. This dialogue invites humanity to witness the world not as a collection of objects, but as a manifestation of the eternal, divine play of Shri Krishna.
