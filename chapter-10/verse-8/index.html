Chapter 10, Verse 8 (Bhagavad Gita 10.8)

Chapter 10: Vibhūti Yog – Yoga through Appreciating the Infinite Opulences of God

Sanskrit Shloka

अहं सर्वस्य प्रभवो मत्तः सर्वं प्रवर्तते। इति मत्वा भजन्ते मां बुधा भावसमन्विताः

Transliteration

ahaṁ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṁ pravartate iti matvā bhajante māṁ budhā bhāva-samanvitāḥ

Word Meanings

aham—I; sarvasya—of all creation; prabhavaḥ—the origin of; mattaḥ—from me; sarvam—everything; pravartate—proceeds; iti—thus; matvā—having known; bhajante—worship; mām—me; budhāḥ—the wise; bhāva-samanvitāḥ—endowed with great faith and devotion

Translation

I am the source of all; from Me everything evolves; understanding this, the wise, endowed with meditation, worship Me with all their hearts.

Meaning & Commentary

In this profound verse, Bhagwan Shri Krishna declares Himself as the ontological foundation of the entire cosmos, the origin from which all existence springs and into which it eventually merges. When a seeker truly realizes that Shri Krishna is the ultimate cause and the indwelling soul of all living beings, their intellectual pursuit transforms into spontaneous devotion. This wisdom acts as the catalyst for a shift in perspective, turning one's daily actions into an act of worship. By recognizing the divinity of Shri Krishna in everything, the devotee attains spiritual equanimity and begins the journey toward liberation. Ultimately, this knowledge allows the soul to dissolve its illusion of separation and rest in the eternal grace of the Supreme Lord.
